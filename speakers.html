Invited speakers

Tuomas Knowles

Professor Tuomas Knowles is a leading expert in physical chemistry and biophysics at the University of Cambridge. Renowned for his pioneering research on protein self-assembly and amyloid diseases, his interdisciplinary work bridges chemistry, biology, and physics. A prolific author and award-winning scientist, Professor Knowles brings profound insights into biomolecular behavior and therapeutic innovation.

For more information, please visit The Knowles lab website

Siân Culley

Dr. Siân Culley is a rising leader in advanced microscopy and quantitative imaging. Based at Kings College London, her research focuses on developing cutting-edge imaging techniques to study cellular processes with unprecedented detail. Dr. Culley's innovative work bridges technology and biology, driving new discoveries in cell biology.

For more information, please visit The Culley Group website

Lisa-Maria Needham

Dr. Lisa-Maria Needham is the Director of the Cambridge Advanced Imaging Centre (CAIC) and the School of Biological Sciences (SBS) Microscopy Network. She specializes in developing advanced microscopy technologies, including ultra-sensitive label-free molecular measurements, custom fluorescent probes, and single-molecule super-resolution imaging, supporting cutting-edge biological research and fostering interdisciplinary collaboration.

For more information, please visit Dr. Lisa-Maria Needham's personal page on CAIC website
